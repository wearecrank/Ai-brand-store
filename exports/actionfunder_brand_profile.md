# Organisation Profile: Actionfunder

## Organisation Info

**Brand Name:** Actionfunder

**Tagline:** The Funding platform revolutionising grant giving

**Industry:** Funding/Grant Management

**Overview:**
ActionFunder streamlines the funding process, cuts costs and amplifies impact. The platform provides everything needed to deliver social value, including local needs analysis, direct messaging, voting tools, live updates, and impact dashboards. It uses AI-driven shortlisting to surface aligned projects tailored to organisational goals. The platform serves as a trusted funding platform for funders and nonprofits, enabling grant programme management, community giving, and social value proof in bids. Features include one-to-one or one-to-many messaging for direct contact with funded groups, real-time project updates with milestones and progress tracking, and comprehensive impact dashboards showing organisation insights and grantee databases.

## Brand Guidelines

**Tone of Voice:**
Warm and accessible, not corporate or distant or cold. The brand feels human, approachable, and genuine with a sense of energy and momentum that drives community action forward.

**Key Words:** warm, accessible, approachable, authentic, energetic

**Typography:** Primary: Inter | Secondary: Roboto Slab Bold (used sparingly for platform-related content) | Inter is used for all headings and body copy. Use Inter Black uppercase for H1 with tight line height (.9/90%). Use Inter Black case-as-typed for H2-H6 with relaxed line height (.4/40%). Roboto Slab Bold is used for headings on platform but should be used sparingly in marketing materials due to its rigid and traditional feel. Roboto Mono Regular is used for meta information such as captions and eyebrows.

**Colour Palette:**
- Navy Blue (#00006B) - Primary brand colour, core identity
- Dusty Pink (#FF79FF) - Primary brand colour, accent and identity
- Deep Black (#00001E) - Text and strong contrast
- White Knight (#FCFCFC) - Neutral background and text
- Muted Pink 500 (#E2B3E4) - Background fills for cards and sections
- Muted Blue 500 (#B8CEDC) - Background fills for cards and sections
- Graphite 500 (#202031) - Tertiary colour for text and elements
- Steel 500 (#D3D4E4) - Tertiary colour for subtle elements

**Brand Dos:**
Prioritise human stories, genuine imagery, and approachable design. Use only brand colours for logo placement. Apply rounded corners to all images. Use hand-drawn elements as ambient design features in the background layer. Maintain proper contrast with text and foreground elements. Use brand fonts and hierarchy consistently in all materials. Create visual hierarchy with clear scannability. Optimise assets for mobile and quick comprehension. Follow image framing guidelines with appropriate corner radius (5px for small, 10px for medium, 20px for large). Use accessible colour combinations that meet 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum (WCAG AA standards). Position logos at horizontal centre, either at top or bottom edge of assets.

**Brand Donts:**
Never use white on pink or pink on white for logo combinations. Do not adjust, rotate, scale or alter the logo. Do not stretch the logo unproportionately. Do not outline the logo. Do not use unsanctioned colours. Do not colour logo elements differently. Do not use poor contrasting colour combinations. Do not use professional composition and immaculate lighting in imagery. Do not use generic stock imagery. Do not use staged photographs unlikely scenarios with no energy or activity. Do not use professionally colour-edited images. Do not use 200/300/600 series colours as primary backgrounds (muted colours only for watermarks on matching 500 backgrounds). Do not mix watermark colours across different colour families. Do not mix consecutive sections with the same background colour in newsletters. Do not use Roboto Slab Bold heavily in marketing materials. Do not make hand-drawn elements the primary focus.
